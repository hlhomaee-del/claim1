Quick Sorting: Rejected vs. Denied

Rejected means the claim could not be processed because required information was missing, invalid, or structurally wrong.

Denied means the claim was processed, but the payer refused payment because of coverage, policy, medical necessity, or code logic.

Fast Reference

Warning: The diagnosis for the visit is not always the patient’s entire medical history. Comorbidities matter only when they affect care, complexity, or medical necessity.

Modifier Reference Desk

Students can consult this while choosing the modifier dropdown. This is intentionally brief so the activity stays fast.

25 — Significant, separately identifiable E/M service on the same day as a procedure.
50 — Bilateral procedure performed on both sides during the same session.
59 — Distinct procedural service; separate site/session/procedure when normally bundled.
RT / LT — Right side / Left side, when laterality must be shown.
None — No special circumstance documented; do not add a modifier just to “make it look complete.”
24 — Unrelated E/M during postoperative period. Use only when documentation supports it.

Case Activities: Predict the Claim Problem

Read the evidence, identify the claim problem, predict the decision, choose the correct next step, and repair the claim. The file names do not give away the answer.

Plan Decision Lab: What Will the Insurance Do?

Now investigate the insurance plan itself. Read the plan evidence, choose the plan type, identify the rule that matters, predict what happens, and decide the next action. After three incorrect attempts, the hint button unlocks. Hints come one at a time so you have to think between attempts.
